What's costing Garage Door Companies money right now

Broken spring calls lost before 8 AM

Torsion springs snap early morning when homeowners try to leave for work. A car trapped behind a jammed door is a panic call — if nobody answers, they Google the next company. Each lost emergency repair is $300-$500 in revenue gone in under 60 seconds.

Dispatch coordination buries your office staff

Between triaging spring vs. opener vs. off-track calls, confirming door sizes and panel counts, coordinating tech routes, and sending arrival-window texts, your dispatcher spends the entire day on the phone instead of selling higher-margin installations.

No maintenance upsells after the repair

Most garage doors need annual lubrication, safety sensor checks, and spring tension inspections. Without automated follow-up, repair-only customers never convert to maintenance plans worth $150-$250 annually — revenue your competitors capture with a simple reminder.
