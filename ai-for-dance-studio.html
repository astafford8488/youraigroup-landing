What's costing Dance Studios money right now

Trial Class Inquiries Lost During Lessons

Parents call while your teachers are in the middle of a class. The phone rings to voicemail, and by evening that family has signed up at a competing studio. At $175 per month tuition plus $1,000 in annual recital and competition fees, each lost dancer costs you over $3,100 per year.

Recital and Costume Admin Overwhelm

Tracking costume orders, recital fees, sizing, and payment deadlines across dozens of families is a full-time job during recital season. Late orders mean rush shipping fees. Missed deadlines mean frantic parents. This admin overload costs $1,500 or more per recital cycle in staff time and rush charges.

Summer Drop-Off Empties Classes

Without automated re-enrollment campaigns, 25 to 35 percent of dancers do not return after the summer break. Losing six students at $175 per month means $12,600 in annual tuition revenue disappears simply because nobody sent a timely re-enrollment reminder before fall registration opened.

It can. AI answers calls from parents asking about ballet, hip-hop, jazz, tap, or any style you offer. It collects the dancer's age, experience level, and class preferences, then books a trial class on the spot. Whether you operate a competitive studio or a recreational program, AI fills your roster without pulling instructors off the floor.

AI answers every call and web inquiry, registers new students for classes, books trial classes, sends class reminders, coordinates recital enrollment and costume ordering deadlines, manages competition scheduling logistics, runs re-enrollment campaigns each season, and requests Google reviews from families. It keeps your front desk organized all year long.
