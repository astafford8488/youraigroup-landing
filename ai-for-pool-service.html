What's costing Pool Service Companies money right now

Green pool calls go unanswered poolside

When a homeowner wakes up to a green pool before a weekend party, they need someone now. If your phone goes to voicemail while you're testing chlorine levels on another route stop, that $200-$500 algae treatment and potential recurring client goes to a competitor.

Route scheduling and chemical logs eat office hours

Between weekly route optimization, new customer onboarding, chemical reading documentation, and equipment repair scheduling, your back office burns 10-15 hours per week on tasks that don't generate revenue but can't be skipped.

Seasonal customers churn without opening reminders

Pool owners who close in October forget who serviced them by April. Without automated spring opening reminders and pre-season equipment checks, you lose $1,200-$3,000 in annual recurring revenue per household to whichever company markets to them first.
